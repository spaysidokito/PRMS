## Background of the Study

Modern student affairs administration demands centralized and efficient digital systems to effectively manage student services, streamline operations, and enhance institutional efficiency. For instance, offices like St. Anne College Lucena's Office of Student Affairs (OSA) encounter difficulties when managing student profiles across spreadsheets, coordinating events through separate calendars, distributing forms via email attachments, and tracking form submissions manually. Without a unified platform, challenges such as data inconsistencies, communication gaps, and redundant manual processes arise. Implementing an integrated Student Affairs Management System addresses these issues by offering a centralized hub that automates workflows, ensures real-time data accuracy, and facilitates seamless operations—ultimately improving the student experience and institutional decision-making (Vidyalaya School Software, 2024; Campus Cafe Software, 2024).

A key challenge in student affairs administration is the reliance on fragmented systems that hinder efficiency and coordination. According to Creatrix Campus, over 70% of institutions face issues due to disconnected tools used for managing student records, event planning, and resource distribution ("Benefits of Student Information System," 2024). Offices often find themselves switching between platforms—such as using Excel spreadsheets for student data, Google Calendar for event scheduling, email for form distribution, and physical filing systems for document storage—which leads to data silos and miscommunication. Integrated Student Affairs Management Systems like PRIMOSA address these inefficiencies by centralizing all student-related operations, offering real-time access to student profiles, event schedules, digital resources, and form submission statuses in a single platform. This not only streamlines workflows but also supports better service delivery through organized record-keeping and efficient resource management ("Student Retention SIS," 2024).

Many student affairs tasks—such as student profile management, event coordination, resource distribution, and form approval workflows—are still performed manually due to the absence of digital automation. This reliance on traditional methods often results in increased workload, delayed responses, and reduced focus on student engagement. This project developed PRIMOSA as an integrated platform to consolidate essential student affairs management functions into a single solution for the Office of Student Affairs (OSA) at St. Anne College Lucena, Inc. The system responds to the growing need for an advanced, unified solution that enhances productivity, enables efficient record management, and streamlines institutional workflows. Traditional approaches have required staff to constantly switch between separate applications for student record management, event scheduling, form distribution, and document storage—a fragmented process that results in operational inefficiencies, data inconsistencies, and workflow disruptions. By combining these critical functions into one cohesive platform, PRIMOSA eliminates these challenges while addressing the unique needs of student affairs management in Philippine higher education institutions.

To address these challenges, PRIMOSA was developed as a Project and Resource Information Management System tailored to the dynamic needs of student affairs operations. Unlike conventional single-purpose applications, PRIMOSA integrates key functionalities—such as student profile management, event coordination, digital resource distribution, form submission workflows, user management, and analytics reporting—into a unified and user-friendly platform. The system features comprehensive student records with profile pictures and academic information, event management with status tracking, digital form distribution for SOA, GTC, and POD documents, form submission and approval workflows, role-based access control, and analytics tools designed to enhance operational transparency and support data-driven decision-making.

## Statement of the Problem

The Office of Student Affairs at St. Anne College Lucena, Inc. faces significant challenges in managing student information, coordinating institutional events, distributing resources, and processing form submissions due to the absence of an integrated digital system. This study seeks to address the following specific problems:

1. How can student profile management be centralized to provide comprehensive, accessible, and up-to-date student records including academic information, contact details, and profile pictures?

2. How can event coordination be streamlined to enable efficient creation, tracking, and status monitoring of institutional activities?

3. How can resource distribution be improved to provide organized access to digital forms such as Statement of Account (SOA), Good Moral Certificate (GTC), and Proof of Delivery (POD) with proper version control and access logging?

4. How can form submission workflows be digitized to enable students to submit forms electronically and staff to review, approve, or reject submissions with proper tracking and notification?

5. How can user management be implemented to provide role-based access control distinguishing between students, faculty/staff, and administrators with appropriate permissions?

6. How can system usage be monitored to provide analytics on resource access patterns, user activity, and institutional statistics for informed decision-making?

7. How can the system ensure security and data protection through proper authentication, authorization, and audit logging mechanisms?

8. How can the interface be designed to provide responsive, accessible, and user-friendly experiences across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices?

## Objectives of the Study

This study aims to develop PRIMOSA, a Project and Resource Information Management System for the Office of Student Affairs at St. Anne College Lucena, Inc., with the following specific objectives:

1. To design and implement a centralized student profile management system that stores comprehensive student information including personal details, academic records, contact information, and profile pictures with search and filter capabilities.

2. To develop an event management module that enables authorized users to create, edit, and track institutional events with status monitoring, venue information, and date scheduling.

3. To create a resource management system that allows staff to upload, organize, and distribute digital forms with preview and download capabilities, version control, and access logging for accountability.

4. To implement a form submission workflow system that enables students to submit forms electronically and provides staff with review, approval, and rejection capabilities with status tracking and notifications.

5. To establish a user management system with role-based access control that distinguishes between students, faculty/staff, and administrators with granular permissions controlling feature access.

6. To develop an analytics dashboard that displays resource access statistics, user activity patterns, and institutional metrics with filtering capabilities and CSV export functionality for reporting.

7. To implement comprehensive security measures including password hashing, CSRF protection, SQL injection prevention, and audit logging to protect data confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

8. To design a responsive user interface that adapts to different screen sizes, providing table layouts for desktop devices and card-based layouts for mobile devices with consistent styling and accessibility features.

9. To evaluate the system's quality, usability, and effectiveness using the ISO/IEC 25010 Software Quality Model through structured feedback from students, faculty, staff, and administrators.

## Scope and Limitations

### Scope

PRIMOSA encompasses the following features and functionalities:

**Student Profile Management** includes creation and maintenance of comprehensive student records with personal information (name, student ID, birth date, gender, contact details), academic information (course, year level, section, department/cluster, enrollment status), profile picture upload and display, search and filter capabilities by department, course, and year level, and account activation/deactivation controls.

**Event Management** provides event creation with title, description, dates, venue, and type specification, status tracking for upcoming, ongoing, and completed events, event listing with filtering and sorting capabilities, event editing and deletion by authorized users, and visibility of event information to all users based on permissions.

**Resource Management** enables upload of digital forms (SOA, GTC, POD) with metadata, organization of resources by form type, preview functionality for document viewing, download capabilities with access logging, version control for updated forms, and permission-based access restrictions.

**Form Submission System** allows students to select form types and upload completed documents, provides submission status tracking (pending, approved, rejected), enables staff review with approval or rejection capabilities, includes comment functionality for feedback, sends email notifications for status changes, and maintains submission history with timestamps and reviewer information.

**User Management** implements role-based access control for students, faculty/staff, and administrators, provides user account creation and editing capabilities, enables account activation and deactivation, includes user listing with filtering by role and status, supports password reset functionality, and assigns granular permissions (canView, canEdit, canDelete, canManageUsers).

**Analytics and Reporting** displays summary statistics for resource access (views, downloads, previews, uploads), identifies top users by access frequency, highlights most accessed forms and resources, provides recent activity timeline with filtering capabilities, enables CSV export for external analysis, and shows real-time statistics for system status.

**Security Features** include bcrypt password hashing, CSRF protection for state-changing requests, SQL injection prevention through parameterized queries, role-based authorization checks, input validation and sanitization, audit logging of user activities, and secure file storage with access controls.

**Responsive Interface** provides desktop layouts with table views and full navigation, tablet layouts with optimized spacing and touch-friendly controls, mobile layouts with card views and hamburger menu navigation, consistent styling across devices, and accessibility features including semantic HTML and keyboard navigation support.

### Limitations

The system has the following limitations:

**Functional Limitations**: PRIMOSA does not include real-time chat or messaging features between users, does not provide task management or project tracking capabilities, does not implement Kanban boards or Gantt charts for planning, does not include automated report generation beyond CSV export, and does not integrate with external systems such as learning management systems or financial systems.

**Technical Limitations**: The system requires internet connectivity for access as it is web-based, depends on browser compatibility (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari), requires adequate server resources for file storage and database operations, and has file upload size limitations based on server configuration.

**Deployment Limitations**: The system is designed specifically for St. Anne College Lucena, Inc. and may require customization for other institutions, is deployed on local infrastructure rather than cloud services, and requires technical expertise for installation, configuration, and maintenance.

**User Limitations**: The system assumes users have basic computer literacy and internet access, requires email addresses for account creation and notifications, and depends on proper role assignment by administrators for appropriate access control.

**Data Limitations**: Historical data from previous systems must be manually migrated, the system does not automatically sync with existing institutional databases, and analytics are limited to data generated within the PRIMOSA system.

Despite these limitations, PRIMOSA effectively addresses the core needs of the Office of Student Affairs at St. Anne College Lucena, Inc., providing a solid foundation for digital student affairs management that can be enhanced and expanded in future iterations.
